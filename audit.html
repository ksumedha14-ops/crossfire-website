The Methodology

7 things we investigate
in every audit

Each point represents a specific visibility lever. Most businesses are losing ground on 3–4 of these simultaneously — and don't know it.

01
Google Business Profile Status
Your GBP is the single most important ranking factor for Maps. An incomplete, inconsistent, or unoptimised profile is the fastest way to invisibility.
  • Completeness score and missing fields
  • Category selection vs. competitors
  • Photo quality, count, and recency
  • Service area configuration
Most common finding: critical categories missing or wrong.
02
Google Maps Ranking Position
Where you rank for each relevant search term — and how far the gap is between you and the top 3.
  • Current position for 8–12 target keywords
  • Local pack inclusion rate
  • Geographic ranking variation
  • Ranking trend (improving or declining)
Most common finding: ranking for wrong keywords, missing high-intent terms.
03
Website Visibility
How your website supports (or undermines) your Maps rankings. NAP consistency, local signals, mobile experience.
  • NAP consistency between site and GBP
  • Local schema markup
  • Mobile page speed (Core Web Vitals)
  • Location page quality
Most common finding: NAP mismatch causing ranking confusion.
04
Competitor Positioning
Why the businesses above you are there — and exactly what they're doing that you're not.
  • Top 3 competitor GBP analysis
  • Review volume and velocity comparison
  • Category and keyword gap analysis
  • Citation profile comparison
Most common finding: competitors aren't untouchable — they just optimised more systematically.
05
Citation Health
Your business information across directories — inconsistencies confuse Google and suppress rankings.
  • NAP consistency across 40+ directories
  • Duplicate listing detection
  • Missing directory coverage
  • Spam signals in citations
Most common finding: 15–30 citation inconsistencies suppressing local rankings.
06
Review Profile
Your reviews as a trust and ranking signal — volume, recency, sentiment, and response quality.
  • Review volume vs. top competitors
  • Review recency and velocity
  • Rating distribution analysis
  • Owner response rate and quality
Most common finding: no system for collecting reviews — relying on word-of-mouth.
07
Growth Opportunity
Based on everything above: your specific opportunity — the gap between where you are and top 3, and what it would realistically take to close it.
  • Realistic timeline to top 3 in your specific market
  • Which 2–3 actions would move the needle fastest
  • Estimated monthly lead increase from top 3 ranking
  • Whether our services are the right fit — and if not, what would be
  • Investment range for your specific situation
This is the section most clients tell us was worth the call alone.
